Thor’s Thunderbread

A hearty, rustic bread with a crackling crust and stormy flavor—worthy of the thunder god himself.

Jump to Recipe
Prep time: 15 mins (plus 2h rise)
Cook time: 40 mins
Servings: 1 large loaf
Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients

  • 3 ½ cups bread flour
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 ½ cups warm water
  • 1 packet (7g) active dry yeast
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Optional: oats, seeds, or coarse salt for topping

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, mix warm water, honey, and yeast. Let sit for 5–10 minutes until foamy.
  2. Add flour, salt, and olive oil to the bowl. Mix until a sticky dough forms.
  3. Knead on a floured surface for 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let rise for about 2 hours or until doubled in size.
  5. Preheat the oven to 220°C (425°F). Place a baking stone or tray inside to heat up.
  6. Punch down the dough and shape into a round or oval loaf. Place on parchment paper.
  7. Optional: Sprinkle the top with oats, seeds, or coarse salt. Score the surface with a sharp knife.
  8. Carefully transfer the loaf (on the parchment) to the hot tray or stone in the oven.
  9. Bake for 35–40 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the loaf sounds hollow when tapped.
  10. Let cool on a wire rack before slicing. Serve with butter, stews, or honeyed mead.

Notes

This bread is thunderous on its own but also perfect for soaking up rich broths and sauces. For an even heartier version, mix in chopped walnuts or roasted garlic to the dough.
